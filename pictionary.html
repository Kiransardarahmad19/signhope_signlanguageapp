ASL Signs: Alphabet A

Aunt

To do aunt, make a fist with your thumb sticking out near your face, at the top of the fist. Leading with the thumb, move your hand in a small circle near your cheek (but not touching it).

Ask

To sign ask, hold up the index finger of your dominant hand in front of your body while the rest of the hand is in a fist, then as you move your index forward, transform it into the letter 'X' sign.It is as if you are turning it into a mini question mark as you talk to the child in front of you.

ASL Signs: Alphabet B

Bathroom

Bathroom is signed the same way as toilet. where we make a letter 'T' with our dominant hand, and we shake it back and forth in front of us.

Bye-Bye

Bye-bye is the same as the traditional gesture for the word. Open your palm, folding down your fingers, then open your palm again.

ASL Signs: Alphabet C

Call

Call is a natural sign. Make the letter Y sign with your dominant hand, and hold your pinkie finger against your chin. (This is also the sign for phone.) Then move your 'Y' hand forward, as if to indicate that you are calling somebody.

Count

To sign count in ASL, your non-dominant hand should be flat with palm facing up to form a base. In your dominant hand, take your thumb and index finger together as if they are making an outline of a coin. Then run your imaginary circle along you non-dominant hand.

ASL Signs: Alphabet D

Dress Up

The sign for dress up or wearing a costume is the same as the sign for clothes. You take both hands open and palms facing down, and brush your thumbs on your chest. To show that you are not wearing normal clothes and that you are playing dress up, you make the motion and your body language more jaunty and up-beat.

Drink

The sign for drink looks just like you are holding and drinking from an imaginary cup. To sign cup, make your hand into a 'C' shape, as if holding a cup in your hand. Take your hand up to your mouth as if drinking from the cup.

ASL Signs: Alphabet E

Everyone

Everyone is a compound sign. First, sign every (which is the same as any), then sign number 1. To sign every, form the ASL letter 'A' sign with both hands aligned in front of you, each lying on the pinkie side of the fist and sticking the thumb out, with the non-dominant hand positioned lower than the dominant one. Keeping your non-dominant 'A' fist in place, motion your dominant 'A' hand downward about half a foot's distance until it disappears behind your other hand - making the sign for any - then finish off by signing '1'.

Everyday

To sign everyday, form your dominant hand into the ASL letter A sign, then brush it against your cheek, starting from your ear all the way past your face a couple of times.

ASL Signs: Alphabet F

Fall

To sign fall or autumn, make your flat, dominant hand move in a circular fashion on the elbow of your non-dominant arm as the palm faces the ground. Clench your non-dominant hand into a fist as your open, dominant hand mimics falling leaves off your non-dominant elbow (which reminds us of a tree branch).

Family

Family is signed by first making the ASL sign for 'F' with both hands, then forming a circle with both hands horizontally, starting near your torso and ending away from your body. Start with the two 'F' hands touching at the index fingers and thumbs, then circle the two hands around horizontally, ending with the pinkie fingers touching, as if you were encircling a group of people in front of you.

ASL Signs: Alphabet G

Girl

Start by making a fist with your thumb sticking out. Take your thumb and, starting at the back of your jaw, run the thumb along your jaw, ending at your chin. The girl sign is like running your thumb along the ribbon on a baby's bonnet.

Good Night

To sign good night, combine the signs for good and night.

ASL Signs: Alphabet H

Happy

Call is a natural sign. Make the letter Y sign with your dominant hand, and hold your pinkie finger against your chin. (This is also the sign for phone.) Then move your 'Y' hand forward, as if to indicate that you are calling somebody.

Help

To sign help, place your closed-fist, dominant 'A' hand on top of your non-dominant open palm, and move both hands upwards.

ASL Signs: Alphabet I

In

The generic sign for in is signed by making your non-dominant hand into a cup shape, then with your dominant hand's fingers touching, place your fingers inside the cup.

iPhone

iPhone is signed a lot like phone, except you don't hold up the letter Y hand to your ear and mouth; you replace the thumb of your 'Y' hand with the letter I sign and hold that up to your ear, while you keep your pinkie finger up against the corner of your mouth.

ASL Signs: Alphabet J

Job

Job has two sign options: either finger-spell the ASL letters J and B (but making sure that the 'B' has your palm facing in this time), or simply make the sign for work.

Juice

To sign juice, extend your pinkie while curling up your three middle fingers. Then lay your thumb down across the fingernails of the three middle fingers. Now take the hand and start the juice sign by pointing your pinkie at your chin with your palm forward. Then rotate your hand so that your palm is now facing forward.

ASL Signs: Alphabet K

Kitchen

To sign kitchen, lay your non-dominant hand in front of you, palm facing up. With your dominant, flat open hand, make a flipping motion above the non-dominant hand. It's as if you're flipping pancakes or eggs on your hand which serves as the frying pan.

Knife

To make the sign for knife, make a knife with the index and middle finger of your dominant hand, and a surface with the same fingers of your non-dominant. Finish the sign by making a back-and-forth cutting movement.

ASL Signs: Alphabet L

Lost

To sign lost, form both hands into a bent shape, with all fingers touching on each respective hand, and the middle knuckles of one bent hand touching the other, as the thumbs stick up front and center. Then separate both hands as you move them downward and away from each other, ending up in a loose, open shape, fingers facing down. It is as if you lost an object that was just in your hands a moment ago.

Listen

Point at your ear with your index finger. Alternatively, you can cup your hand to your ear as if you are straining to hear or listen.

ASL Signs: Alphabet M

Mad

To sign mad, make an angry face and bring a scary-looking claw toward your face. To make the claw, use your dominant hand and spread your fingers while curving them.

Medicine

Medicine is signed by holding your non-dominant hand with palm facing up. Now place then middle finger of your dominant hand in the center of your non-dominant palm, and make a twisting motion with the former, as if you're crushing a tablet or pill on your palm.

ASL Signs: Alphabet N

Near

Start with your two hands out in front of your body, with your palms flat and facing your body. Place your dominant hand a little in front of your non-dominant hand. Then bring your dominant hand toward your dominant hand, bringing it near your body, in effect.

New

The sign for new looks like you are scooping or shoveling up a new plant from the ground. Your non-dominant hand is flat, palm facing up, to signify the ground. Your dominant hand is a curved open palm that makes a scooping motion into the "ground" and ends facing up and above a short distance from the ground.

ASL Signs: Alphabet O

Off

To sign off, hold your non-dominant hand horizontally, with your palm down. Put your dominant hand on top of your other hand, and tilt it up so that it goes from being horizontal to vertical. Bend your dominant forearm at a right angle. The sign is like your hands are a switch moving to the off position.

On

To sign on, start with your non-dominant hand horizontal, palm facing down, and your dominant hand up at a right angle. Lower the dominant hand so that it becomes flat on top of the non-dominant hand. It is like you have a switch and are lowering it onto the on position.

ASL Signs: Alphabet P

People

People is signed a lot like visit, but instead of using 'V' hands, you use 'P' hands. First, form both hands into the ASL letter P sign. Then move the hands in alternate circles: up, forward, down, and back. When the right hand is forward, the left hand is back. When the right hand is up, the left hand is down.

Please

To sign please, take your dominant hand with fingers extended and held together, and thumb extended and sticking out. Take the hand with palm facing in and rub it in a circle on your chest.

ASL Signs: Alphabet Q

Quiet

cross your flat, open hands and then release them for the second part of the sign.

Question

Question is a natural sign. Simply take your index finger and trace a big question mark in the air in front of you.

ASL Signs: Alphabet R

Rain

To sign rain, open up both hands, and starting with your hands above your head, bring them down. Your fingers are like the raindrops falling down.

Run

To sign run, position both lateral L hands in the sign for fast then hook them together, one hand in front of the other. Do a fast-forward, pointer-wriggling motion with the leading hand that conveys movement.

ASL Signs: Alphabet S

Sad

The sad sign looks like you are making tears fall down your face. Take both hands with fingers outstretched, and pull them from above your eyes down to about chest level. With emotional signs such as sad, you should make facial expressions that match the sign. Make a droopy face with downcast eyes while you make the sad sign.

See

To sign see, make a 'V' with your dominant hand, and place your index finger under your dominant eye. Point your 'V' hand toward the listener or the object that you “see”.

ASL Signs: Alphabet T

Taste

Taste is signed by tapping your middle finger on your chin (or on your lips, as some people do). You take an open palm, indent your middle finger slightly, and tap it on your chin or lips.

Thank You

To sign thank you, extend your fingers and thumb. Touch you fingers to your chin and bring your fingers forward. It is almost like you are blowing a kiss out, to thank the person - but the sign is a bit lower.

ASL Signs: Alphabet U

Up

The sign for up is made by taking your index finger and aiming it skyward. Raise and lower your arm so it is like you are pointing up at the sky.

Umbrella

To sign umbrella, start with two fists stacked on top of each other with your dominant hand on top. Raise the top fist. The sign looks like you are opening an umbrella.

ASL Signs: Alphabet V

Visit

To do the baby sign for visit, form both hands into the ASL sign for letter 'V'. Move the hands in alternate circles: up, forward, down, and back. When the right hand is forward the left hand is back. When the right hand is up, the left hand is down.

Village

Village is signed exactly like city, community, and town: take both slightly curved flat hands, then have them touch at the fingertips while facing opposite directions. Switch both hands' positions while twisting them back and forth, releasing and touching the fingertips a couple of times.

ASL Signs: Alphabet W

Wait

To sign wait, hold up both your open hands, palms facing up and fingers spread out Then wiggle your fingers as you simultaneously make circular movements with both open hands, up and away a short distance, then down and toward your body again. Repeat the sequence.

Walk

The walk sign looks like two feet walking along the ground. To make the baby sign for walk, take both hands with fingers extended and held together on each hand. Position the palms facing the ground and move one palm up, forward and down. Then move the other palm up, forward and down.

ASL Signs: Alphabet X

X-Ray

To do the baby sign for x-ray, make the ASL sign for the letter 'X' with your dominant hand at your opposite shoulder, then bring it to your chest a short diagonal distance down.

Xerox

To sign Xerox, hold your non-dominant forearm horizontally in front of your body, your hand flat and facing down. Then form your dominant hand into the ASL letter X sign, palm side facing down, and starting from underneath your non-dominant forearm, slide your 'X' hand back and forth. Your 'X' hand is mimicking the scanning function of your photocopier.

ASL Signs: Alphabet Y

Yesterday

To sign yesterday, form a modified ASL letter A sign, but with your thumb sticking out more prominently from the rest of the fist. Touch your thumb to the side of your chin, then move it backward to touch your jawbone.

You're Welcome

You’re welcome is signed the same way as Thank you. Take your flat, open, dominant hand, and starting from your chin, take your hand out as it arcs down and away from your face. It is as if you are acknowledging that your baby has thanked you, so you are mirroring the polite gesture back, like thanking them for thanking you!

ASL Signs: Alphabet Z

Zipper

Zipper is a natural sign. Take both hands and pinch the thumb to the index finger on either hand. Your non-dominant hand stays put at the imagined location of the zipper, while the dominant hand makes the motion of unzipping and zipping from the "base" where the non-dominant hand is positioned.

Zoo

Zoo is signed by outlining the word Z - O - O. First, you outline a 'Z' in the air with your index finger. Then you make an 'O' shape with your index finger and thumb, and move the hand sideways across your body. (To do any double letter in ASL, the specific letter sign is moved across the body, as if to indicate an extension or repetition of that letter.)
